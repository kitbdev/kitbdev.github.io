Development Interests

  • VR Interactions

    Interacting with things in VR is part of what makes it fun. There are so many possibilites in this space, and many new paradigms to discover. I want to create systems that players find intuitive and easy to use.

  • VR Immersion

    Putting players in a detailed space increases immersion, but VR can do so much more than that. Seamless transitions, good performance, and responsive environments are important to make the player believe they are somewhere else. When I make a VR experience, I want the player to feel physically present in this virtual world.

  • Shaders

    Both believability and interesting game mechanics may depend on the visual look and feel of the experience. Shaders allow us to make these complex effects and have them suit our needs. I am familiar with Unity's Shader Graph and shader fundamentals to be able to control these impactful effects.

  • Procedural Generation

    Generation of content at runtime is a powerful tool. It can be used for practically infinite content, reducing size requirements, and creating new experiences with each run. Procedural Generation gives us the ability to create things that otherwise may be impossible. I enjoy creating the kinds of advanced algorithms that let us make useful content and I strive to make it coherent and practical.
