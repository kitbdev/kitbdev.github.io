Game Jams

What is a Game Jam?
Game Jams are events where participants make a video game from scratch in a limited time frame. Often game jams will have required themes or limitations, ratings, and a community to chat with other participants.

Why?
Creating an entire game from scratch in a short time frames gives valuable experience for all aspects of production, including time management, planning, implementation, scope management, polishing, publishing, marketing, and just all around development experience.

Some Notes
Where the Wall opens was made in a group of 9 individuals in the Central Florida Region of Global Game Jam 2021. I was the lead developer and in charge of enemy AI, menu functionality, subtitles, and various gameplay logic. We used Unity Collaborate so the lead designer, the other developer, and myself could work in Unity together.
All other projects were made entirely by me. This includes code, textures, models, sound effects, and music unless otherwise stated.
Most jams were made in under 72 hours, but a few were made within a one or two weeks. (Disc Room Game Jam, 7 Day Roguelike, Global Game Jam)
