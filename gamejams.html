Game Jams

Game Jams are events where participants make a video game from scratch in a limited time frame. Often they will have required themes or limitations, ratings, and a community to chat with other participants.

Creating an entire game from scratch in such a short time frames gives valuable experience for all aspects of production, including time management, planning, implementation, scope management, polishing, publishing, marketing, and just all around development experience.
Game Jams help you learn to prototype in quick iterations and give an understanding of game design and what makes games fun.
It takes lots of skills to complete a Game Jam. Modelling, texturing, rigging, animating, game design, level design, programming systems, UI design, system balancing, and playtesting are just some of the hats needed.
Even though it can be hard, I like Game Jams because I love creating games and they give me the chance to express myself in a unique way.

All projects (except Where The Wall Opens) were made entirely and solely by me. This includes code, textures, models, sound effects, and music unless otherwise stated. Most jams were made in under 72 hours, but a few were made within a one or two weeks. Jams made in a week include Disc Room Game Jam, 7 Day Roguelike, and Global Game Jam.

Where The Wall Opens was made in a group of 9 individuals in the Central Florida Region of Global Game Jam 2021. I was the lead developer and in charge of enemy AI, menu functionality, subtitles, and various gameplay logic. There was a lead designer, lead writer, lead developer (me!), another developer, and the rest were artists, musicians, and voice actors.
I enjoyed woking on a game with a team, it was fun to talk with others and work toward the same goal. It lifted some responsibilities I'm not the best at, like music and character design, and it let me focus more on what I can do best, like programming and engineering.
